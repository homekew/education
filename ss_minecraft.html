8th Grade Role
Historian & Focal Builder
You research, plan, and build the centerpiece structure each week — the longhouse, the fort, the courthouse, the cannery. Your build is the landmark that defines the era. You also write a short Build Brief each week telling your partner what to build around yours, and keep a running History Log in Google Docs.
6th Grade Role
Community Builder & Chronicler
You read the brief and build the world around the centerpiece — the homes, paths, farms, markets, and everyday details that bring the place to life. Your builds give context to your partner's landmarks. Each week you write a short Build Report in Google Docs.
Semester Vocabulary
indigenous — originally from a place; Oregon's Native peoples have lived here 15,000+ years
sovereignty — the right of a people to govern themselves and control their land
primary source — a document created at the time of an event (journal, letter, map)
settlement — when people move to and occupy a new place, often displacing those already there
statehood — when a territory officially becomes a U.S. state (Oregon: 1859)
perspective — a point of view shaped by who you are and what you've experienced
Unit 1 · Weeks 1–4

The First Oregonians

Oregon's Native nations have lived here for at least 15,000 years. Before the world grows, you need to understand who this land belongs to. This unit maps the tribes, studies their architecture and lifestyles, and builds an Indigenous Oregon across three biomes — coast, valley, and plateau.

W1Who Lived Here First — Oregon's Native Nations & the Chinook
Mon Scout the world together · 8th: map tribes and plan build sites · 6th: read overview ▾
8th Grade — Focal Builder
  1. Research Oregon's tribal territories — search "Oregon tribal nations map." List the 9 major nations and which region each comes from (coast, valley, plateau, basin).
  2. Open Minecraft together and scout for three biomes: coastal area (Chinook), flat inland valley (Kalapuya), desert/mesa (plateau). You call out what you see — your partner navigates.
  3. Mark each site with a different colored block.
History Log Entry #1

Oregon has [number] recognized tribal nations. The ones I hadn't heard of before today include [names]. The region with the most tribes is [region] because [geography reason].

3 sentences. Typed in Google Docs.

✓ Done when: 9 tribes listed + log entry written + three build sites marked in Minecraft
6th Grade — Community Builder
  1. Read the overview your teacher gives you about Oregon's first peoples (1–2 paragraphs or 3–5 min video).
  2. Write 2 sentences in your Build Log: one thing that surprised you, one thing you already knew.
  3. Open Minecraft with your partner. Navigate to each biome they call out and help place the marker blocks.
✓ Done when: log entry written + three sites flagged together

  3. Write one sentence in your History Log about what you're trying to represent.
✓ Done when: research done + longhouse frame started in Minecraft + log note written

4–5 sentences. Typed and shared with partner.

W3The Plateau & Desert — Paiute, Cayuse & Nez Perce
Mon–Tue 8th: Research plateau tribes + build main tipi · 6th: Clear desert site + gather materials ▾
8th Grade — Focal Builder
  1. Research the Northern Paiute (Great Basin) and Cayuse/Nez Perce (Columbia Plateau). How did horses change plateau life after the 1700s? What did their camps look like — tipis, mobile, near water?

History Log — Before & After Horses

Before horses reached the plateau (~1700), the Cayuse traveled on foot and hunted [animals] by [method]. After horses arrived, they could [new capability], which changed [aspect of life] because [reason].

4 sentences (2 before, 2 after).

✓ Done when: research done + main tipi built + log entry written

2–3 sentences per perspective + 1 connecting sentence.

Unit 2 · Weeks 5–8

Exploration & Change

Lewis and Clark arrive in 1805. The Oregon Trail brings thousands of settlers in the 1840s. For Oregon's Native peoples, this era brings displacement, conflict, and loss. This unit adds the era of exploration and early settlement to your world — and asks hard questions about whose story gets told.

W5Lewis & Clark Reach Oregon — 1805
Mon 8th: Read journal excerpts + start Fort Clatsop · 6th: Build the Columbia River ▾
8th Grade — Focal Builder
  1. Read 2–3 short excerpts from the Lewis and Clark journals describing Oregon (lewisandclarkjournals.unl.edu or teacher's packet). Note: they were not discovering land — people already lived there. How do they describe the Native peoples they meet?
  2. In Minecraft, go to the coastal site and start building Fort Clatsop — the log fort where they wintered in 1805–06. Square log walls with a gate, simple interior rooms. Start the frame today.
  3. Write 2 History Log sentences: what Lewis and Clark found + one sentence about how they described the Native peoples.
✓ Done when: journals read + Fort Clatsop frame started + log sentences written
6th Grade — Community Builder
  1. Look at a map of the Lewis and Clark route through Oregon — they came down the Columbia River to the coast.
  2. In Minecraft, build/extend the river system to clearly represent the Columbia. Make it wide and prominent. Add a sign: "Columbia River."
  3. Clear a flat area near the Chinook village for the fort your partner is building — it will go right next to the existing village.
✓ Done when: Columbia River built + fort site cleared
Tue–Thu 8th: Finish Fort Clatsop + write Two Voices · 6th: Add trail camp + build brief followed ▾
8th Grade — Focal Builder
  1. Finish Fort Clatsop — complete the log walls, add a gate, interior rooms (sleeping bunks as slabs, a storage room, a fire pit in the center). Add a sign: "Fort Clatsop — Winter 1805–06."
  2. Write a "Two Voices" History Log entry: what did the expedition mean from Lewis & Clark's point of view vs. the Clatsop people's point of view?
  3. Write Build Brief #5 for your partner: a few smaller explorer camp structures to add nearby (campfire, supply tent, observation post).
Two Voices Template

Lewis & Clark's perspective: For the Corps, reaching the Pacific meant [significance]. They saw Oregon as [description].

Clatsop perspective: For the Clatsop people, this arrival meant [impact]. They had [context], and now [what changed].

2–3 sentences per voice.

✓ Done when: Fort Clatsop complete + Two Voices written + Build Brief #5 shared
6th Grade — Community Builder

Unit 3 · Weeks 9–12

Oregon Grows

Oregon becomes a state in 1859. Industries emerge — logging, fishing, farming. Cities grow. By the end of this unit your Minecraft world spans 15,000 years of Oregon history, from the first peoples to the present day.

W9Statehood — Oregon Joins the Union (1859)
Mon–Tue 8th: Research statehood + build courthouse · 6th: Build the town around it ▾
8th Grade — Focal Builder

  2. In Minecraft, build the 1859 courthouse — your centerpiece: stone brick walls, a bell tower, columns on the front (pillar blocks), a sign: "Oregon Territorial Courthouse — 1859." Make it the largest and most prominent building in the new town area.
  3. Write Build Brief #9 for your partner: the pioneer town around the courthouse.
History Log — Oregon 1859

When Oregon became a state in 1859, one thing it got right was [positive aspect]. However, one thing it got wrong was [specific wrong — exclusion laws, treatment of Native peoples]. This matters today because [connection to the present].

3 sentences. Be specific.

✓ Done when: courthouse built + log entry written + Build Brief #9 shared
6th Grade — Community Builder

  3. Add a sign: "Oregon City — Statehood 1859."
